7 Things to Check Before Choosing a Pneumatic Actuator for Oil and Gas Projects

The wrong actuator in an oil and gas application does not just fail. It fails expensively, dangerously, and at the worst possible moment.

1. Operating Pressure Range

Check minimum and maximum working pressure ratings carefully. Factor in real-world pressure drops across your pneumatic supply lines.

2. Torque Output and Valve Compatibility

A safety factor of 25 to 50 percent above required torque is standard practice for good reason.

3. Materials and Corrosion Resistance

Oil and gas environments are chemically aggressive. Pay particular attention to seal materials.

4-7. Additional Checks
